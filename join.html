Join NEES Lab

Build technology with human impact.

We welcome motivated students interested in embedded computing, biosignals, machine learning, rehabilitation, and human–machine interfaces.

Who should reach out?

Potential projects often combine several areas. You do not need to arrive with experience in all of them, but you should be curious, dependable, and willing to learn across disciplinary boundaries.

Computing

C/C++, Python, mobile development, machine learning, signal processing, or data visualization.

Hardware

Microcontrollers, embedded Linux, sensors, PCB design, robotics, prototyping, or 3D printing.

How to inquire

  1. Read our research page.
    Identify one or two themes that genuinely interest you.
  2. Prepare a short introduction.
    Include your program, year, relevant courses, skills, and what you hope to learn.
  3. Attach supporting material.
    A résumé and an unofficial transcript help provide context. A project portfolio or GitHub link is welcome.
  4. Email Professor Zhang.
    Use a clear subject such as “NEES Lab research inquiry — Your Name.”

Research culture

Learn by making, testing, and sharing.

Students contribute to prototypes, experiments, analysis, papers, posters, and demonstrations. Project availability depends on current funding, mentoring capacity, and preparation.
